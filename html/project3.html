Why My Favorite Dinosaur Is a Stegosaurus

The stegosaurus is my favorite dinosaur because of how all around cool it is, in lack of terms. It's not a predator, but it also isn't afraid to swing its weight around to defend itself. While it's usually seen as a “friendly herbivore,” it was definitely capable of doing serious damage to anything that messed with it.

What Makes Stegosaurus Special?

One of the most recognizable things about the stegosaurus is the huge plates going down its back, and honestly that’s probably the first thing anyone notices about it. these plates could get pretty big and were lined up in two rows, which made the stegosaurus look a lot larger and more intimidating than it actually was. scientists don’t fully agree on what they were used for, but some think they helped with temperature control by releasing heat, while others think they were more for display, like scaring off predators or just making the stegosaurus look more impressive to others of its kind. either way, they weren’t just random decorations, they actually had a purpose.

Then there’s its tail, which had four long spikes. this weapon is actually nicknamed a “thagomizer,” and it wasn’t just for show either. the stegosaurus could swing its tail with a lot of force if it needed to defend itself, and fossil evidence shows that these spikes were strong enough to seriously injure predators like allosaurus. there are even fossils that show damage that matches the shape of those spikes, which basically proves it actually used them in fights. so even though it wasn’t a predator, it definitely wasn’t helpless either, and could do some real damage if something tried to attack it.

Interesting Facts About Stegosaurus

  • It lived about 150 million years ago during the Late Jurassic period.
  • It could grow up to around 30 feet long and weighed several tons.
  • Its brain was very small compared to its body, about the size of a walnut.
  • Despite that, it still had strong survival instincts and defenses.
  • Its tail spikes (thagomizer) could be used like a weapon against predators.
  • The plates on its back may have helped regulate body temperature or for display.
